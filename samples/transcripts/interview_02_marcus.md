In-Depth Interview – Marcus Webb

Age: 26
Generation: Gen Z
Location: Boulder, CO
Occupation: Bike Mechanic
Device: Garmin Forerunner 55
Family: Single

1. BACKGROUND

INTERVIEWER: Walk me through a typical week for you.

MARCUS: Work five days at the shop, ride four or five. Trail running two mornings a week before we open. So I'm outside a lot, and I'm hard on gear. Everything I own is scratched.

INTERVIEWER: How does that shape what you buy?

MARCUS: I buy for durability first. I've broken three phones. I don't buy pretty things, I buy things that survive.

2. CURRENT SOLUTION

INTERVIEWER: You've got a Forerunner. How's that going?

MARCUS: It's good for running. It's the wrong tool for what I do now. No maps, and the battery's fine for an hour but not for a full day out. I've had it three years and I'm at the point where I want the next thing.

INTERVIEWER: What's stopping you?

MARCUS: Sticker shock, mostly. The Fenix line is what I actually want and it's genuinely more than my monthly rent contribution. I keep waiting for it to go on sale and it never really does.

3. PAINS

INTERVIEWER: What's the most frustrating part?

MARCUS: The jump. There's the cheap running watch I have, and then there's the eight-hundred-dollar expedition watch, and there's basically nothing between them. It feels deliberate. Like they want you to either be a casual or a fanatic and there's no room to be a person who's just... outside a lot.

INTERVIEWER: Does anything else bother you?

MARCUS: Software bloat. My watch wants to tell me my "body battery" and my stress score and I don't care. Give me GPS, give me elevation, give me a week of battery. Stop trying to be my therapist.

4. GAINS

INTERVIEWER: What would the right product unlock for you?

MARCUS: Confidence to go bigger. There are routes I've mapped out that are eight, nine hours, and I don't do them because if I roll an ankle out there with a dead watch, nobody's finding me. That's not paranoia, that's just the math.

5. OBJECTIONS

INTERVIEWER: If a three-hundred-dollar Garmin with real GPS and a week of battery existed, what's your reaction?

MARCUS: Suspicion, first. Then I'd go read the forums for a month. I'd want to know the screen isn't garbage and the strap doesn't fail, because that's where they always cut costs. The strap is always where they cut costs.

INTERVIEWER: What would change your mind?

MARCUS: Teardowns. Someone opening it up and showing me it's the same GPS module as the expensive one. And a real warranty. If they stand behind it for two years I'll believe them.

6. BRAND PERCEPTION

INTERVIEWER: What do you think of Garmin?

MARCUS: They make the best hardware and the worst software, and they charge like they know it. But in the shop, when someone asks me what to buy, I still say Garmin. That tells you something.

7. VOCABULARY

INTERVIEWER: How would you describe the gap you're seeing?

MARCUS: The missing middle. That's what I'd call it. Everything's either a toy or a tool for someone getting paid to be outside.
