In-Depth Interview – Dani Okafor

Age: 21
Generation: Gen Z
Location: Austin, TX
Occupation: Student, part-time barista
Device: Fitbit Charge 5
Family: Lives with parents

1. BACKGROUND

INTERVIEWER: Tell me what you're into.

DANI: I got into trail running last year, which I did not expect. I was a very indoor person. My friend dragged me out to Barton Creek and now I'm doing it three, four times a week. It's the only thing that shuts my brain up.

INTERVIEWER: What are you using to track it?

DANI: A Fitbit. It was a gift. It's fine? It counts steps. It thinks I'm asleep when I'm sitting still. The GPS takes like four minutes to find me and sometimes it just doesn't.

2. CURRENT SOLUTION

INTERVIEWER: Have you looked at upgrading?

DANI: I look constantly. It's a whole thing, I have a tab open right now. But I'm a student. Three hundred dollars is a lot of money. Six hundred is not a real number to me, it's fictional.

3. PAINS

INTERVIEWER: What bothers you about your current setup?

DANI: I don't trust it. I finished a run last week and it told me I'd gone 4.1 miles and I know for a fact it was closer to six because I've run that loop fifty times. So what am I even tracking? I'm tracking a guess.

INTERVIEWER: Does that matter to you?

DANI: It matters a lot, actually. The whole reason I keep going is seeing myself get better. If the numbers are made up, the progress is made up.

4. GAINS

INTERVIEWER: What would you want instead?

DANI: Something accurate that doesn't look like medical equipment. I want to wear it every day. I want it to look good with what I'm wearing. That sounds shallow but I'm twenty-one and I'm going to wear this thing to class.

5. OBJECTIONS

INTERVIEWER: What would stop you from buying a Garmin at three hundred?

DANI: Three hundred is still three hundred. I'd have to save for it, and while I'm saving I'd second-guess it. I'd want to know it lasts. Like, is this a four-year watch or a two-year watch? Because at two years that's a subscription and I can't do that.

INTERVIEWER: What would change your mind?

DANI: Honestly, if I could try it. Or a really good return window. The risk is what stops me, not the price exactly. It's the risk of spending three hundred dollars on something I end up not liking.

6. BRAND PERCEPTION

INTERVIEWER: What do you think of Garmin?

DANI: I think of my uncle. That's the honest answer. It's a brand for serious people and I don't feel serious yet, even though I run more than most people I know.

7. VOCABULARY

INTERVIEWER: How do you talk about this with your friends?

DANI: I say I want a watch that "actually knows where I am." That's the bar. It's a low bar and nothing under three hundred dollars is clearing it.
